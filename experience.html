Experience

Student, Warehouse Management, Sales, Manual Labor, Entrepreneurship

As a student at Brigham Young University, I have gained valuable experiences that have shaped my work ethic and understanding of various business sectors. Previously, I worked as a Warehouse Manager for Blue Coolers, where I honed my skills in shipping and receiving freight, managing inventory, and delivering exceptional customer service. This role taught me the importance of efficient operations and effective communication. I also served as a Sales Representative for Caddis Sports, wholesaling outdoor gear to retail stores, which enhanced my sales and relationship-building abilities. Additionally, my high school experience in a wood shop, where I used nail guns to assemble wooden boxes, instilled a strong foundation in manual labor and craftsmanship. I also recently ventured into entrepreneurship by starting my own company, Graham Promos, specializing in company swag and promotional gear. These diverse experiences have not only equipped me with practical skills but also fueled my ambition to succeed in the business world.

My LDS mission in Sierra Leone and Seattle, Washington, profoundly changed my life in numerous ways. In Sierra Leone, I immersed myself in a diverse culture, learning resilience and empathy while serving communities in need. The challenges of navigating a different environment taught me the importance of adaptability and strengthened my faith. Following that experience, my time in Seattle exposed me to a vibrant urban landscape, allowing me to connect with people from various backgrounds and share messages of hope and service. This dual experience not only deepened my understanding of different cultures but also reinforced my commitment to service, compassion, and personal growth, shaping me into a more thoughtful and dedicated individual.
